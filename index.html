FAQ

Asked on almost every audit call.

Do I have to leave FishingBooker right away?

No. Most captains keep their listings while we build direct bookings up. As your own site takes over, the platforms become optional overflow instead of your lifeline — and you decide when to cut them loose.

Who owns the website and the customer list?

You do. Your domain, your Stripe account, your customer data. If we ever part ways, everything stays with you. That's the whole point of getting off the platforms.

How do payments actually work?

Bookings run through Stripe connected to your own bank account. You set deposit rules (50% is typical), customers pay by card or Apple Pay, and payouts land in your account in about two business days. You pay only standard card processing — no commission to us or anyone else.

What if a trip gets weathered out?

The booking system handles reschedules and refunds in a couple of clicks, and automated texts offer your next open dates first — so a blown day turns into a moved booking instead of a lost one.

How much does it cost?

It depends on whether you need the full build or just ads on an existing site. On the audit call we'll show you the math against what you're currently paying in commissions — for most boats running 10+ trips a month, the switch pays for itself.

Do you work with charters outside Florida?

We're built in St. Pete and know these waters best, but the system works anywhere anglers search Google. If you run a legitimate charter operation, book the call.
